# Theme 5: Undersea Laboratory

**Aesthetic:** Deep-sea research station built from pressure-rated hulls, thick glass viewports, and bioluminescent tech. Barnacles and coral creep onto older equipment. A mix of industrial diving hardware and alien ocean biology. Every component feels like it's operating under immense pressure — because it is.

**Palette:** Deep navy/teal hull plating, rust-orange corrosion accents, pale green bioluminescence, brass dive-hardware fittings, cyan bioluminescent glow for highlights (health layer).

**Shared traits:** Every component is a pressure-sealed module on a coral-encrusted seafloor base plate. Thick riveted hulls with circular glass portholes. Cyan bioluminescent organisms (jellyfish tendrils, glowing algae, deep-sea fish) serve as the health layer. Bubbles rise from active components.

---

## Components

### Server
A diving bell compute pod — a brass and steel bell-shaped pressure vessel with a thick glass porthole showing spinning mechanisms inside. Bubbles stream from the exhaust valve on top. Barnacles on the lower hull.

### Database
A sunken treasure chest in a pressure case — reinforced glass and steel box containing data cores styled as gold bars/artifacts. A mechanical claw arm for retrieval sits on top. Cyan anemones grow along the edges.

### Data Cache
A jellyfish relay — a bioluminescent jellyfish tethered in a glass containment sphere. Its tendrils pulse cyan when active, delivering cached data through bio-electrical signals. Small and fast.

### Load Balancer
An octopus distribution hub — a mechanical octopus sculpture with brass tentacles, each gripping a different pipe/conduit. The octopus rotates to direct flow. Cyan bioluminescence along each tentacle.

### CDN
A sonar buoy — an anchored buoy with a hydrophone array that broadcasts data as sound waves. Cyan sonar rings pulse outward. A small antenna pokes above the implied water surface.

### API Gateway
A submarine airlock — heavy circular hatch door with a hand wheel, pressure gauge, and status indicator. Must equalize pressure (authenticate) before opening. Cyan pressure seal ring around the door.

### Queue
A torpedo tube rack — cylindrical loading tubes with data torpedoes queued up nose-to-tail. A mechanical loading arm pushes the next one into position. Cyan warhead tips glow when ready.

### Worker
A mechanical crab — a brass automaton crab with tool-arms (wrench, welder) actively working on something. Cyan eyes and joint lights. Scuttles across the seafloor processing tasks. Sparks from the tool arm.

### Streaming Server
A whale song broadcaster — a large horn/speaker module shaped like a whale's mouth, continuously emitting cyan sound waves. Bio-mechanical hybrid — half machine, half organism. Constant output.

### Edge Cache
A hermit crab cache — a small mobile shell housing (like a hermit crab's borrowed shell) with data stored inside. Sits closer to the action than the main storage. Cyan shell markings. Portable and fast.

### DNS/GTM
A nautical chart table — a waterproof map case with ocean current charts. Mechanical compass and sextant mounted on top. Cyan current lines show optimal routing paths across the sea floor.

### Blob Storage
A deep-sea trench vault — a massive pressure door set into a rock face / trench wall. Industrial scale — holds the biggest artifacts. Cyan deep-sea fish circle the entrance. Warning depth markers on the frame.

### Circuit Breaker
A pressure relief valve — a large emergency blow valve on a ballast tank. When pressure exceeds limits, it releases a massive burst of cyan bubbles. Red emergency handle. Depth gauge shows danger zone.
